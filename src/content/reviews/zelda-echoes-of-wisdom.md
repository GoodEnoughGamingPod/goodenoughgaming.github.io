For decades, we've played as Link to save Zelda. Now the tables have turned, and the princess proves she's more than capable of carrying her own game.

## Gameplay

Echoes of Wisdom's core mechanic — creating "echoes" of objects and enemies you've encountered — is brilliantly inventive. It turns every encounter into a puzzle with multiple solutions. Want to fight that Moblin head-on? Sure. Or you could drop a boulder on it. Or summon your own Moblin to do the work for you.

## Visually Stunning

The Link's Awakening art style returns and it looks gorgeous. Every area is bursting with colour and personality, making exploration a genuine delight.

## Where It Falls Short

The difficulty curve is a bit uneven. Some dungeons feel too easy once you've collected enough powerful echoes, while a few boss fights spike unexpectedly. The camera can also be finicky in tight spaces.

## Verdict

Echoes of Wisdom is a refreshing entry in the Zelda franchise. It takes creative risks that mostly pay off and gives Zelda the starring role she's always deserved.

**Rating: 8/10**
